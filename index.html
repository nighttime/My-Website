about me
Hi. I’m a Ph.D. candidate in the Institute for Language, Cognition, and Computation at the University of Edinburgh. I did my B.Sc. in Computer Science at Brown University. I research techniques in natural language inference (NLI), especially to do with tasks like question answering, and data in the form of structured knowledge (Knowledge Graphs, Entailment Graphs) or unstructured, open-domain natural language predicates. I'm also interested in representation learning and latent structure in Language Model embedding space. I am not a member of the Church of Bertology (joke). I've also made some creative things, like games, apps, and generative art. Check out a sample of projects below.
